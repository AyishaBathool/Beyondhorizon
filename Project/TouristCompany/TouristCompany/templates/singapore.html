Top Places to Visit in Singapore

Marina Bay Sands

This iconic hotel and entertainment complex is famous for its stunning rooftop infinity pool, observation deck with panoramic views of the city, and a wide range of dining and shopping options.

  • Year Established: Marina Bay Sands opened in 2010.
  • Iconic Architecture: Known for its unique architecture, it features three towers with a rooftop park connecting them.
  • Architect: Moshe Safdie
  • Hotel Rooms: Over 2,500 luxurious guest rooms and suites.

If you're planning to visit the Marina Bay Sands, consider the following tips:

  • Marina Bay Sands has several sustainability initiatives, such as water and energy conservation.
  • The light and sound show at the Marina Bay Sands Skypark is a must-see attraction.
  • Features a permanent art collection, including works by renowned artists.

Gardens by the Bay

TThese futuristic gardens are home to the impressive Supertree Grove, Cloud Forest, and Flower Dome. It's a beautiful place to explore nature and experience sustainable horticulture.

  • Year Established: 2012.
  • Location: Marina Bay, Singapore.
  • Total Area: 101 hectares (250 acres).
  • Architect: Grant Associates (landscape) and Wilkinson Eyre (Supertree Grove).

When visiting the Gardens by the Bay:

  • The Heritage Gardens showcase Singapore's cultural diversity.
  • The Cloud Forest features a unique display of rare and high-altitude plants.
  • The Gardens are known for their sustainability efforts and environmentally-friendly features.

Sentosa Island

This resort island offers a variety of attractions, including Universal Studios Singapore, Adventure Cove Waterpark, S.E.A. Aquarium, and beautiful beaches.

  • Year Established: Developed as a resort in the 1970s.
  • Location: It is located in the southern part of Singapore, connected to the mainland by a causeway.
  • Architect: Sentosa's development involved various architects and urban planners over the years.
  • Area: Sentosa Island covers approximately 5.5 square kilometers (2.1 square miles).

When visiting the Sentosa Island:

  • Sentosa boasts several beautiful beaches, including Siloso Beach, Palawan Beach, and Tanjong Beach.
  • Universal Studios Singapore: This popular theme park opened on Sentosa Island in 2010 and features numerous rides and attractions based on Hollywood movies.
  • Sentosa offers several nature trails and lush green spaces for relaxation.

Singapore Zoo

Renowned for its open and spacious enclosures, the Singapore Zoo offers the opportunity to see a wide variety of animals in a more naturalistic setting.

  • Year Established: 1973
  • Area: Approximately 69 acres (28 hectares)
  • Number of Visitors (annually): Over 2 million.
  • World's First: Known for being the world's first "open concept" zoo.

When visiting the Singapore Zoo:

  • Guided tram rides for an overview of the zoo.
  • Features a water playground and animal-themed carousel.
  • Received numerous awards for its conservation efforts.

Singapore Botanic Gardens

This UNESCO World Heritage site features lush greenery, colorful orchids in the National Orchid Garden, and a peaceful atmosphere for a leisurely stroll.

  • Year Established: 1859
  • Architect: Lawrence Niven
  • Area: 74 hectares (183 acres)
  • Number of Visitors (annually): Over 4.5 million.

When visiting the Singapore Botanic Gardens:

  • Numerous themed gardens like the Healing Garden and Fragrant Garden.
  • Offers guided tours for visitors interested in the garden's history and biodiversity.
  • A popular spot for picnics and leisurely walks.

Chinatown

Explore the vibrant streets of Chinatown with its bustling markets, historic temples like the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple, and delicious street food.

  • Year Established: 1822.
  • Architectural Style: A blend of Chinese and colonial influences.
  • Area: Approximately 42 hectares.
  • Location: In the heart of Singapore's Central Business District.

When visiting the Chinatown:

  • Immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of Chinatown through guided tours and cultural performances.
  • Renowned for its delicious street food, including Hainanese chicken rice, dim sum, and more.
  • Visit during Chinese New Year for festive celebrations.

Little India

Immerse yourself in Indian culture with a visit to Little India. You'll find colorful streets, aromatic spices, and ornate temples like Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple.

  • Year Established: Early 19th century.
  • Architectural Style: Reflects traditional Indian architecture.
  • Area: Approximately 40 hectares.
  • Number of Visitors (Annually): Over 2 million tourists.

When visiting the Little India:

  • Little India in Singapore is a vibrant and culturally rich neighborhood that offers a slice of Indian heritage, cuisine, and traditions amidst the modern cityscape.
  • Showcases the history and culture of the Indian community in Singapore.
  • Opportunities to engage in traditional music and dance performances.

Merlion Park

Home to the iconic Merlion statue, this park offers picturesque views of the city skyline and Marina Bay. It's a great spot for photos.

  • Year Established: 1972.
  • Architect: Fraser Brunner.
  • Area: 2,500 square meters.
  • Symbol of Singapore: The Merlion is a national symbol of Singapore.

When visiting the Merlion Park:

  • A popular place to view fireworks during New Year's Eve celebrations.
  • Features well-maintained walking paths along the waterfront.

ArtScience Museum

Located at Marina Bay Sands, this museum combines art and science in interactive exhibitions and is known for its unique architecture.

  • Year Established: 2011.
  • Architect: Moshe Safdie, a renowned architect known for his innovative designs.
  • Area: The museum is situated within the iconic Marina Bay Sands resort complex.
  • The museum's unique lotus-shaped building is a prominent feature of Singapore's skyline.

When visiting the ArtScience Museum:

  • Popular tourist exhibitions have included "Future World: Where Art Meets Science" and "Treasures of the Natural World."
  • The structure is known for its sustainable design, featuring rainwater harvesting and energy-efficient systems.
  • ArtScience Museum is a must-visit destination for those interested in the fusion of art and science, and it adds a unique dimension to Singapore's cultural landscape.

National Museum of Singapore

Dive into the country's history and culture with a visit to this museum, which offers engaging exhibits and multimedia displays.

  • Year Established: The National Museum of Singapore was established in 1849.
  • Architect: It was designed by architect John Frederick Adolphus McNair.
  • Area: The museum is located on Stamford Road and occupies an area of approximately 18,400 square meters.
  • Number of Visitors: The museum welcomes over 1.5 million visitors annually.

When visiting the National Museum of Singapore:

  • The museum offers interactive displays and multimedia presentations to engage visitors.
  • It is known for its commitment to the conservation and preservation of Singapore's heritage.
  • The National Museum of Singapore is a key institution for understanding Singapore's rich history and culture, making it a must-visit for tourists interested in the country's heritage.
